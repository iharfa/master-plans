How the documents fit together

The Urban Development Framework is the top-level policy layer: 8 Policy Pillars → 33 Strategic Objectives → 133 Strategic Policy Directions. Those 8 pillars map one-to-one onto the 8 themes of the Urban Development Master Plan booklet. The four sector master plans (Social, Transport, Environment, Landscape/Garden Island) hold the delivering actions. Alignment means every policy direction has an action feeding it, no two plans quietly duplicate or contradict each other, and the wording stays consistent as documents are revised.

Coverage by pillar

Share of each pillar's strategic objectives that at least one plan currently contributes an action toward.

This is a structural / textual alignment check, not an engineering or legal review. The keyword engine (see Upload & Versions) is deliberately transparent and conservative — treat its output as a prompt for human review, and read the curated Gaps and Overlaps tabs for the substantive findings.

Coverage matrix — pillars × plans

Which plan owns or contributes to each framework pillar. A pillar with only secondary contributions and no primary owner is a candidate for a gap.

Coverage gaps

Strategic Policy Directions with little or no delivering action in any master plan. Each is flagged with the recommended fix and whether it should be resolved at the master-plan level.

Overlaps & required realignment

Where two or more plans address the same topic and the text needs to be coordinated. Each card names which documents to change and how to divide ownership.

Document integrity issues

Internal errors — numbering, typos, and missing links — found while parsing the source documents. These are quick corrections independent of the alignment findings.

Framework explorer

The full framework, pillar by pillar, showing the contributing plans against every objective. Click a pillar to expand. Objectives with no contributing plan are flagged GAP.

Upload updated plans & track versions

Drop in revised master-plan .md files. The tool re-runs the coverage logic, compares against the previous run, and saves a version so you can see whether an edit closed a gap or opened a new one. Everything runs in your browser — nothing is uploaded to a server.

How to read the engine. It scans each plan for the distinctive signal phrases of the 8 tracked gaps. A match means the gap topic now appears in the text — which is a prompt to confirm it is a real delivering action, not just narrative wording. The authoritative, action-level judgement stays the curated 76% on the other tabs; the engine's job here is to show what changed between revisions and version-track it.
